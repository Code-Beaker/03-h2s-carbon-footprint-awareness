Climate 101

Understanding Our Impact

Before we measure our footprint, let's explore how our daily actions shape the atmosphere and how simple changes can heal the planet.

1. The Invisible Trail

Every time we drive, use electricity, buy food, or throw away trash, we release greenhouse gases into the air. This total volume of emissions is your carbon footprint—the invisible trail we leave behind.

Tap to reveal a quick fact

Fact: The global average individual footprint is about 4.7 tonnes of CO₂ per year, equivalent to burning over 5,000 pounds of coal.

2. The Greenhouse Blanket

Greenhouse gases act like a thick blanket around Earth, trapping the sun's heat. This warming causes severe droughts, rising sea levels, and extreme storms that threaten our communities and ecosystems.

Tap to reveal a quick fact

Fact: Earth's average temperature has risen by 1.1°C since the late 19th century, driving more extreme and frequent heatwaves.

3. The Power of Choice

The solution is simple: reduce emissions by making cleaner choices. Transitioning to renewable energy, walking instead of driving, eating local food, and composting can turn the tide.

Tap to reveal a quick fact

Fact: Small shifts count. Simply setting your AC to 24°C or using public transit twice a week saves over 300 kg of CO₂ annually.
